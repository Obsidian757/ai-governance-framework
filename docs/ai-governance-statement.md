## Our AI Governance Principles

### 1. Human-Centric AI
We design and recommend AI systems that augment human decision-making, not replace human judgment. Every AI engagement we deliver includes meaningful human oversight.

### 2. Transparency & Explainability
We document how AI systems work, what data they use, and what limitations they have. Clients receive clear explanations of AI-driven outputs we recommend or build.

### 3. Security First
Our founder holds an active DoD Secret clearance and brings two decades of U.S. Navy and defense-systems experience. We apply defense-grade security thinking to AI implementations and prefer zero-trust architecture by default.

### 4. Compliance by Design
We build regulatory requirements (NIST AI RMF, OMB M-25-21 / M-25-22, EO 14110, Virginia EO-30, VITA AI Policy Standard, EA-225, ISO/IEC 42001:2023) into AI system planning from day one — not as an afterthought.

### 5. Continuous Accountability
Every engagement has a named owner. Every recommendation is traceable to a documented control. Every issue is investigated and learned from.

## How We Recommend Clients Manage AI Risk

### Before Deployment
- AI impact assessment for every system
- Bias and fairness review
- Security and privacy review
- Compliance validation against federal and (where applicable) Commonwealth of Virginia requirements

### During Operation
- Performance and accuracy monitoring with defined thresholds (EA-225 alignment)
- Periodic bias review
- Security assessments on the cadence the engagement scope defines
- Incident response with documented escalation

### Continuous Improvement
- Periodic governance review
- Policy updates as standards evolve (e.g., successor Virginia EOs, OMB updates)
- Lessons learned applied to subsequent engagements

These cadences are designed *into client engagements* per scope; they are not blanket SLAs.

For our own internal work, we use industry-recognized large language model platforms and cloud AI services under those vendors' standard enterprise terms.
